ITERATIVE DESIGN

Part 1: Sketching and Wireframing

Attain is a marketplace that acts as a one-stop shop for convenience store owners to purchase inventory from a variety of suppliers.

For this assignment, we're designing a Desktop interface. This will be used by broadly three sets of users:

  • The first will be store owners (retailers) who will use the website to access a marketplace from which to order inventory. Additionally, the interface will sync with their in-store inventory system and provide store-owners with information about what inventory they currently have at their store.
  • The second will be suppliers who list their items on Attain to be sold. They will use the website to add/remove items, keep track of orders, and access analytics about their sales.
  • The third will be users that have not yet signed up to Attain. They might use the interface to create an account (as either a supplier or retailer) or to generally find out more information about the company.

Crit Feedback
  1. Home Page

    • It should be made clear that the section titled “Restocking made Easier” applies to retailers
      • Added text in the section indicating that the section is “For Retailers”
    • Since there will presumably be more retailers visiting the website than suppliers, the order of both the sections should be swapped
      • Placed the “For Retailers” section above the “For Suppliers” Section
  2. Login Page

    • Realign the “Learn More” button to be consistently left aligned with the text above it
      • Moved the “Learn More” button to match the alignment of the above text
  3. Marketplace

    • Placement of the shopping cart icon is not consistent with the placement on the individual item page
      • Moved shopping cart icon to the top right to correspond with the individual item page
    • Next to each item in the marketplace, we earlier had a plus arrow to directly add the item to your cart without visiting the individual item page. We received feedback that this was confusing since it wasn’t clear at what price the item was being added (since the price varies according to quantity)
      • Removed this button – you now have to visit the individual item page to add it to your cart
    • We previously did not have any text with the offers on the marketplace – it was not clear that they were bundles/offers
      • Added text below each promotion indicating what it stands for
  4. Individual Item (Chobani) Page

    • We previously did not display the volume/quantity that each unit of Chobani corresponded to
      • Added the volume per unit (5.3 oz) to the item description
    • Add functionality so that you can type in the number of units of the item you want to buy
      • Added a blinking cursor to make clear that text in the unit quantity box can be edited by typing
    • It wasn’t clear what is the current price per unit or delivery time that is being applied
      • Highlighted the row with the current unit price and delivery time that is being applied

Part 3: User Testing

Tasks
  • Figure out how to sign up as a new retailer. [Success: Yes, No] [5-point rating scale: Very difficult to Very easy]
  • Find the Chobani Yogurt Item on the Marketplace. [Success: Yes, No] [5-point rating scale: Very difficult to Very easy]
  • Find the cost of 8 units of Chobani Strawberry Yoghurt. [Success: Yes, No] [5-point rating scale: Very difficult to Very easy]
  • Could you find if any Promotions would be applicable on the order of 8 Chobani Strawberry Yogurts? [Success: Yes, No] [5-point rating scale: Very difficult to Very easy]
Observations
  1. User 1 (Link to video)
    • User 1 had a relatively easy time working through the steps, they missed the sign-up button in the top right but found the retailer button further down the page. Their biggest piece of feedback was to make the discounts more noticeable on the product page.
  2. User 2 (Link to video)
    • User 2 got slightly confused at the login page because there was already information filled in the login box. Similar to User 1 they wished that the promotions were more visible.
  3. User 3(Link to video)
    • User 3 flowed through the steps easily. They didn’t have any big complaints, however they commented that an additional signup button would be helpful.
Analysis

Our figma design worked, for the most part, as planned. Users were able to navigate through our sign up and select and understand merchandise offered. Moving forward we should probably advertise and better highlight the promotions on the site, they were quite far down on the product page. Also, including a sign up button along with sign in at the top right might better meet users expectations and garner more clicks than embedding it lower down the page.

Thank you!
